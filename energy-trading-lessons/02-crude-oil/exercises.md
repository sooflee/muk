# Module 02 — Exercises

## Exercise 1: WTI-Brent Spread Analysis
- [ ] Pull 2 years of daily WTI and Brent front-month settlement prices
- [ ] Plot the spread (WTI minus Brent) over time
- [ ] Identify 3 periods where the spread moved significantly — research what caused each move
- [ ] Write a paragraph for each: what happened, why, and how a trader could have positioned

## Exercise 2: OPEC Decision Impact
- [ ] Find the last 5 OPEC+ meeting dates and decisions (OPEC website or news archives)
- [ ] For each meeting, note: the decision (cut/hold/increase), the market expectation, and the price move in the 24 hours after
- [ ] Was the market reaction driven by the decision itself or the deviation from expectations?

## Exercise 3: Cushing Storage
- [ ] Find the weekly Cushing crude inventory data (EIA website)
- [ ] Plot Cushing inventories vs. WTI front-month price for the past year
- [ ] What is the correlation? Does a Cushing build always mean lower WTI?

## Exercise 4: Forward Curve Shape
- [ ] Pull the full WTI futures curve (all listed months)
- [ ] Plot it. Is it in contango or backwardation?
- [ ] Calculate the 1st-2nd month spread and the 1st-12th month spread
- [ ] Compare the current curve shape to one year ago — what changed and why?

## Exercise 5: April 2020 Case Study
- [ ] Research what happened to WTI on April 20, 2020 (price went negative)
- [ ] Write a 1-page analysis covering: what caused it, who was hurt, what the storage situation was, and what lessons traders learned
- [ ] Could this happen again? Under what conditions?

## Exercise 6: Grade Differentials
- [ ] Find the current price differential between WTI and at least 2 other crude grades (Mars, LLS, WCS, or Urals)
- [ ] Explain why each grade trades at a premium or discount to WTI
- [ ] What would cause the WTI-Mars spread to widen or narrow?
