# Module 10 — Exercises

## Exercise 1: Write 3 Trade Idea Memos
- [ ] Using the trade memo template from the lesson, write 3 complete trade ideas:
  - One calendar spread (any commodity)
  - One crack or spark spread
  - One basis or inter-commodity spread
- [ ] For each: include fundamental thesis, quantitative support, catalysts, risks, and sizing
- [ ] Get feedback from someone in the industry if possible

## Exercise 2: Paper Trading Portfolio
- [ ] Start a paper trading portfolio with a hypothetical $10M capital base
- [ ] Implement your 3 trade ideas from Exercise 1
- [ ] Track daily P&L, portfolio VaR, and margin usage
- [ ] Run for 4 weeks. Journal every decision: entries, exits, adjustments, and why.
- [ ] At the end, calculate: total return, Sharpe, max drawdown, best and worst trade

## Exercise 3: Weekly Market Commentary
- [ ] Write a 1-page weekly market commentary for 4 consecutive weeks covering:
  - Key price moves (crude, gas, products, power)
  - EIA report analysis
  - Relevant news and events
  - Your outlook for the coming week
- [ ] Practice being concise and opinionated — funds want conviction, not wishy-washy hedging

## Exercise 4: Mock Interview Preparation
- [ ] Prepare answers to all the interview questions listed in the lesson
- [ ] For "give me a trade idea": prepare a 2-minute verbal pitch for each of your 3 trade memos
- [ ] For "what happened this week": practice summarizing the energy market week in 60 seconds
- [ ] Record yourself and review — are you clear, concise, and confident?

## Exercise 5: Fund Research
- [ ] Research 5 energy-focused hedge funds or commodity trading firms
- [ ] For each, find: strategy type, AUM (if available), key people, recent hires, and any public commentary
- [ ] Which firm best matches your interests and strengths?
- [ ] Identify 3 people at each firm to connect with on LinkedIn

## Exercise 6: Build Your Edge
- [ ] Reflect on what unique advantage you could bring to an energy trading desk
- [ ] What data source, analytical technique, or market niche could you specialize in?
- [ ] Write a 1-page "investment philosophy" document: your approach to markets, risk, and idea generation
- [ ] This document will evolve — revisit it after 6 months of studying and paper trading

## Final Capstone: End-to-End Analysis
- [ ] Pick one energy market event from the past 2 years
- [ ] Write a full 3-5 page report covering:
  - What happened (timeline, data, price action)
  - Why it happened (fundamental drivers)
  - Who won and who lost
  - How you would have traded it (with sizing and risk management)
  - What the market got wrong (if anything)
  - What lessons apply going forward
- [ ] This is your portfolio piece — make it sharp enough to show in an interview
