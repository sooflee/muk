# Module 01 — Foundations of Energy Markets

## Overview

Energy is the largest commodity market in the world. Before trading any single product, you need a mental map of how the whole system fits together.

## Key Concepts

### 1. The Energy Value Chain

```
Exploration → Production → Transportation → Refining/Processing → Distribution → Consumption
```

Every step in this chain creates a market. Traders profit by understanding inefficiencies, bottlenecks, and price dislocations between steps.

### 2. Who Are the Market Participants?

- **Producers** — oil majors (ExxonMobil, Shell, Chevron), E&P companies, national oil companies (Saudi Aramco, ADNOC)
- **Consumers/End users** — airlines (jet fuel), utilities (natural gas, coal), industrials
- **Physical traders** — Vitol, Trafigura, Glencore, Gunvor, Mercuria. They move actual cargoes.
- **Banks** — Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan (commodities desks)
- **Hedge funds** — Citadel, Millennium, Andurand, Freepoint. Speculative + relative value
- **Market makers** — provide liquidity on exchanges
- **Exchanges** — CME/NYMEX (US), ICE (global), TOCOM (Asia)

### 3. Physical vs. Financial Markets

**Physical**: Actual barrels of oil, MMBtu of gas, MWh of electricity being delivered somewhere. Governed by logistics — pipelines, tankers, rail, storage.

**Financial**: Futures, options, swaps that reference a physical commodity but settle in cash. Most hedge fund trading is here, but understanding physical flows is what gives you edge.

### 4. Benchmarks — Why They Matter

A benchmark is a standardized reference price. The entire market prices off benchmarks:

| Commodity | Benchmark | Exchange |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| US Crude | WTI (Cushing, OK) | NYMEX |
| Global Crude | Brent (North Sea) | ICE |
| US Natural Gas | Henry Hub (LA) | NYMEX |
| EU Natural Gas | TTF (Netherlands) | ICE |
| Gasoline | RBOB | NYMEX |
| Heating Oil / Diesel | NY Harbor ULSD | NYMEX |

### 5. What Drives Energy Prices?

**Supply side**: OPEC+ decisions, US shale production, geopolitics, sanctions, infrastructure outages, weather (hurricanes in Gulf of Mexico)

**Demand side**: Economic growth (GDP), weather (heating/cooling demand), seasonality (driving season, winter), industrial activity, policy (EV adoption, carbon regulation)

**Inventory/Storage**: The bridge between supply and demand. Inventory builds = bearish. Draws = bullish. The rate of change matters more than the absolute level.

**Forward curve shape**: Contango (future > spot) vs. backwardation (spot > future). This reflects the market's view on supply/demand balance over time.

### 6. Units You Need to Know

| Unit | Used For |
|------|----------|
| Barrel (bbl) — 42 US gallons | Crude oil, refined products |
| MMBtu (million BTU) | Natural gas |
| MWh (megawatt-hour) | Electricity |
| Metric ton (MT) | LNG, coal, physical cargoes |
| $/gallon | Retail gasoline, RBOB |
