# Module 01 — Exercises

## Exercise 1: Map the Market
- [ ] Draw a diagram of the energy value chain from wellhead to gas station
- [ ] For each step, write down who the key players are and what risks they face
- [ ] Identify where a trader could add value or find an edge at each step

## Exercise 2: Know Your Benchmarks
- [ ] Look up the current price of WTI, Brent, Henry Hub, RBOB, and ULSD
- [ ] Calculate the WTI-Brent spread. Is it positive or negative? Why?
- [ ] Find the current RBOB crack spread (RBOB price in $/bbl minus WTI)

## Exercise 3: Read the EIA Report
- [ ] Go to eia.gov and find the latest Weekly Petroleum Status Report
- [ ] Write down: crude inventories (vs. 5-year avg), gasoline inventories, refinery utilization %
- [ ] Was the crude draw/build above or below consensus? What happened to price after the release?

## Exercise 4: Understand the Forward Curve
- [ ] Pull up the WTI futures curve (CME website or any charting tool)
- [ ] Is the market in contango or backwardation?
- [ ] Write 2-3 sentences explaining what the curve shape tells you about current market conditions

## Exercise 5: News Analysis
- [ ] Read energy headlines for one week (Platts, Argus, or Bloomberg)
- [ ] Categorize each story as supply-side, demand-side, or policy/geopolitical
- [ ] Pick one story and write a paragraph on how it could affect prices and which commodity it impacts most

## Exercise 6: Unit Conversions
- [ ] Convert 1 barrel of crude oil to gallons, liters, and metric tons (use API gravity ~35)
- [ ] If Henry Hub gas is $3.50/MMBtu, what is that in $/MWh? (hint: 1 MWh ≈ 3.412 MMBtu)
- [ ] If Brent is $85/bbl and the $/EUR rate is 1.08, what is Brent in EUR/bbl?
