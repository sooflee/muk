# Module 04 — Exercises

## Exercise 1: Calculate Crack Spreads
- [ ] Pull daily WTI, RBOB, and ULSD front-month prices for the past 2 years
- [ ] Convert RBOB and ULSD from $/gallon to $/barrel (multiply by 42)
- [ ] Calculate the 3:2:1 crack spread for each day and plot it
- [ ] What was the average? The max? When did it peak and why?

## Exercise 2: Seasonal Patterns in Gasoline
- [ ] Create a seasonal chart of the RBOB crack spread (overlay 5 years)
- [ ] When does the gasoline crack typically start rising in the spring?
- [ ] When does it typically peak?
- [ ] How would you position a calendar spread to capture this seasonality?

## Exercise 3: Refinery Turnaround Impact
- [ ] Find the EIA weekly refinery utilization data
- [ ] Plot utilization alongside the 3:2:1 crack spread
- [ ] Does lower utilization correspond to wider cracks? What's the lag?

## Exercise 4: Gas vs. Diesel Spread
- [ ] Calculate the RBOB minus ULSD spread for the past 3 years
- [ ] Plot it alongside a measure of economic activity (e.g., trucking index or PMI)
- [ ] When does diesel outperform gasoline? What drives that?

## Exercise 5: 2022 Diesel Spike Case Study
- [ ] Research the diesel/distillate price spike in 2022
- [ ] What caused distillate inventories to fall so low?
- [ ] How did the ULSD crack spread behave? What was the peak?
- [ ] Write a 1-page analysis of the causes and market impact

## Exercise 6: Product Inventory Analysis
- [ ] From the EIA weekly report, chart gasoline and distillate inventories vs. 5-year range
- [ ] Which product is currently tighter relative to historical norms?
- [ ] Based on your analysis, which crack spread should be wider and why?
