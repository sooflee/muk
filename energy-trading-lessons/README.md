# Energy Commodities Trading — Self-Study Curriculum

A structured learning path for breaking into energy trading at a hedge fund.

## How to Use This

- Work through the modules in order (01 through 10)
- Each module has a `lesson.md` with concepts and a `exercises.md` with tasks
- Spend roughly 1-2 weeks per module depending on depth
- The later modules assume you've internalized the earlier ones
- Build your Python analysis toolkit as you go (module 07 onward)

## Modules

| # | Module | Focus |
|---|--------|-------|
| 01 | Foundations | Energy markets overview, key players, market structure |
| 02 | Crude Oil | WTI, Brent, global flows, OPEC, benchmarks |
| 03 | Natural Gas | Henry Hub, TTF, LNG, seasonality, storage |
| 04 | Refined Products | Gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, refining economics |
| 05 | Power Markets | Electricity pricing, ISOs, renewables impact |
| 06 | Trading Mechanics | Futures, options, OTC, clearing, margin |
| 07 | Quantitative Skills | Python, data sources, time series, backtesting |
| 08 | Spreads & Structures | Calendar, crack, spark, locational spreads |
| 09 | Risk Management | VaR, Greeks, correlation, position sizing |
| 10 | Portfolio & Strategy | Fund structures, idea generation, interview prep |

## Daily Habits to Build

- Read the EIA weekly petroleum status report (Wednesdays)
- Read the EIA weekly natural gas storage report (Thursdays)
- Follow S&P Global Platts and Argus headlines
- Check CME/NYMEX front-month energy prices daily
- Read one RBN Energy blog post per day
