# Module 06 — Exercises

## Exercise 1: Contract Math
- [ ] If you are long 10 WTI futures and the price moves from $78.50 to $79.25, what is your P&L?
- [ ] If you are short 5 Henry Hub futures and gas drops from $3.45 to $3.22, what is your P&L?
- [ ] What is the notional value of 1 WTI contract at $80/bbl?
- [ ] If initial margin is $6,000 per WTI contract, what is your leverage ratio at $80/bbl?

## Exercise 2: Forward Curve Construction
- [ ] Pull all listed WTI futures settlement prices (CME website)
- [ ] Plot the full forward curve
- [ ] Calculate the 1-2 month spread, 1-6 month spread, and 1-12 month spread
- [ ] Identify any kinks or inflection points in the curve — what might cause them?

## Exercise 3: Roll Analysis
- [ ] Pull front-month WTI prices and 2nd-month prices for 3 years
- [ ] Calculate the monthly roll yield (sell front, buy 2nd month)
- [ ] What was the cumulative roll cost/gain over the period?
- [ ] How does the roll yield compare in contango vs. backwardation regimes?

## Exercise 4: Options Basics
- [ ] Look up the current WTI options chain for the front month on CME
- [ ] Find the at-the-money call and put. What are their premiums?
- [ ] Are calls or puts more expensive (is there skew)?
- [ ] Calculate the breakeven price for buying the ATM call

## Exercise 5: COT Report Analysis
- [ ] Pull the CFTC Commitments of Traders report for WTI crude oil
- [ ] Track the net speculative position (non-commercial long minus short) over 1 year
- [ ] Does extreme speculative positioning predict price reversals?
- [ ] Write up your findings

## Exercise 6: Month Codes Quiz
- [ ] Without looking: What months are F, H, K, N, U, Z?
- [ ] Decode these contracts: CLZ26, NGH27, RBK26
- [ ] Write out the month code for: WTI July 2027, Gasoline March 2026, Gas December 2026
